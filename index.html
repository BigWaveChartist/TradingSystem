01 · Foundation

The Philosophy Behind the System

Why environment always comes before stock selection.

You can buy a great stock in a poor environment and struggle, or buy an average stock in a strong environment and do very well. The market backdrop is the single most important variable — and it must be assessed before anything else.

Most traders spend the majority of their energy searching for the "perfect" stock. They obsess over earnings, charts, and catalysts while treating market conditions as an afterthought. This system inverts that priority. Before a single stock is evaluated, the overall market environment is assessed and classified. Only once the environment is deemed favorable does the process shift toward individual setups.

The system operates across four core pillars, each building on the last:

  1. Market Health — Is the environment favorable for momentum trading at all?
  2. Setup — Has the stock broken out and entered its most explosive phase, surfing the 8 EMA? Or is it gapping up on a powerful catalyst (EP)?
  3. Entry Execution — Where exactly is the optimal entry point for this setup?
  4. Risk Management — How much to risk, how to size the position, and when to exit?

When all four pillars align, you are trading with maximum probability in your favor. The discipline of this framework lies not in finding more trades, but in having the patience to wait for the conditions where every pillar confirms the same direction.

02 · Market Health

Measuring the Market Environment

Using QQQE as a broad participation gauge — not just price, but internal health.

The market health framework is built around QQQE — the Invesco NASDAQ 100 Equal Weighted ETF. Because it equal-weights all components rather than cap-weighting them, QQQE reveals whether the market's strength is broad or dangerously narrow.

Cap-weighted indices like QQQ can mask a deteriorating market when a handful of mega-cap stocks carry the load. QQQE strips that illusion away. When QQQE is strong, it means the average stock in the Nasdaq 100 is participating — and that is the environment where momentum strategies produce their best results.

Seven specific questions are asked of QQQE and the market each day. The answers feed into a color-coded dashboard called the Trend M.A.P.

  • 01
    Is the QQQE McClellan Summation Index in an Uptrend? The master breadth signal. An uptrending Summation Index confirms that more QQQE components are advancing than declining over time — persistent, broad participation that supports every long setup.
  • 02
    Is QQQE Above a Rising 5 SMA? The very short-term trend filter. Price above a rising 5-day SMA means the market is acting strong immediately — a green light for short-term entries and a warning to avoid when violated.
  • 03
    Daily Buy Signal: QQQE above 10/20 SMA with 10 SMA > 20 SMA Daily timeframe confirmation. This bullish moving average stack filters out choppy regimes and confirms a constructive environment where pullbacks get bought and breakouts extend.
  • 04
    Weekly Buy Signal: QQQE above 10/20 WMA with 10 WMA > 20 WMA The structural uptrend filter. When weekly and daily trends agree, multi-day to multi-week trades have a much more favorable backdrop. This also informs position sizing decisions.
  • 05
    Have I Gained Traction on My Last Few Trades? Self-feedback as a market signal. If your edge is stable but results deteriorate, the problem is usually the environment. A string of clean winners signals it's time to press; repeated stalls signal to step back.
  • 06
    Is QQQE Net 52-Week High/Low Indicator Trending Positive? Leadership expansion check. When more stocks break to new highs than new lows, breakouts have much higher probability of continuing — a hallmark of genuine bull environments.
  • 07
    Is the McClellan Oscillator Out of Overbought Territory? Short-term timing filter. Extreme breadth readings can signal near-term exhaustion. Once the oscillator resets from overbought while the broader trend remains bullish, entry quality improves significantly.

Key Insight: Items 1–5 directly drive the Trend M.A.P. signal color. Items 6 and 7 are tracked daily as important context but don't change the overall dashboard color — they inform the quality of any given environment beyond the primary signal.

04 · EMA Structure

Surfing the 8 EMA

The most explosive phase of any great momentum stock's run — and how to enter it with discipline.

Once a leading stock breaks out with conviction, the strongest of them rarely look back. Instead, they attach themselves to the 8 EMA — a fast-moving average that tracks their steepening trajectory with precision. This is the behavioral signature of a true liquid leader in full markup phase.

The angle of ascent is sharper. Pullbacks are shallower and shorter-lived. Whereas the stock once needed several weeks to absorb selling pressure before resuming higher, it now bounces back within days — sometimes within a single session. The 8 EMA becomes the new equilibrium point between buyers and sellers, and the sellers are clearly losing the argument.

What drives this behavior is institutional urgency. When a stock is in genuine price discovery, large funds that missed the initial breakout are now chasing exposure. Every minor dip becomes a buying opportunity for managers who are underweight the name. This creates a self-reinforcing dynamic where pullbacks to the 8 EMA are shallow because the demand response is both fast and substantial.

The most powerful momentum stocks — the ones that double or triple in a matter of months — will surf this 8 EMA structure for the bulk of their move. They'll touch it, or come close to it, and then accelerate higher again. This pattern can repeat six, eight, even ten or more times over the course of a major run, with each test representing a fresh opportunity.

The 8 EMA Entry Framework

Entering After the Breakout is Confirmed

Rather than chasing a stock extended well above all moving averages, the 8 EMA pullback entry provides a structured, logical path in. The stock has already demonstrated institutional backing — the breakout itself is confirmation. The pullback to the 8 EMA is an opportunity to enter a proven leader at a moment of temporary, natural digestion.

  • Entry after the move is confirmed — reduced uncertainty vs. chasing
  • Stop at a close below the 8 EMA structure low
  • The first pullback after breakout is the most powerful
  • Can repeat 6–10+ times across the full run
  • Risk/reward: tight stop, defined support, unlimited upside

The 8 EMA Pullback Checklist: Volume should dry up as the stock approaches the average — light volume on the pullback signals sellers aren't motivated and the stock is resting, not being distributed. Daily ranges should compress, not expand. A clean test on contracting volume followed by a resumption day on expanding volume is one of the highest-probability entry setups in momentum trading. If the stock closes below the 8 EMA structure low, the phase may be over — exit and wait for the next setup.

Stop-Loss Management on 8 EMA Entries

The initial soft stop-loss on an 8 EMA structure entry is placed at the structure low. This is a logical and defined level: if the stock undercuts it, the structure has failed and the trade thesis is invalidated.

As the trade develops and the structure continues moving upward, the stop naturally rises with it — closing the gap between cost-basis and the stop organically, without any forced mechanical adjustment. The structure itself does the work of tightening risk over time.

For ongoing position management, the 10 EMA becomes the trailing guide. Two consecutive daily closes below the 10 EMA signal that the stock's momentum has meaningfully shifted — at that point, part or all of the position is sold. This two-close rule prevents premature exits on single-day violations while still responding promptly when the trend genuinely softens.

The Entry Extension Limit

Just as EP trades use a 60% ATR filter to avoid chasing intraday, 8 EMA structure entries have their own extension rule: do not enter more than 0.5 ATR above the 8 EMA structure high. If the stock has already bounced strongly off the 8 EMA and is now extended beyond that threshold, the entry no longer offers a favorable risk/reward. The appropriate response is to wait — either for the next pullback to the 8 EMA, or for the stock to consolidate and offer a fresh, tight setup. Buying extension is how clean setups become frustrating losses.

Finding candidates for the 8 EMA swing trade requires a different scan than chasing pre-market gappers. The goal here is to surface stocks that are already in strong technical shape — trading above their 50-day SMA, within striking distance of 52-week highs, with enough average volume and a price above $10 to ensure real liquidity and institutional interest.

This screener runs daily and forms the foundation of the swing trade watchlist. The scan deliberately excludes biotech and sectors prone to binary headline risk, keeping the focus on liquid, trending names with durable momentum.

Average volume over 200K guarantees the liquidity needed to enter and exit without slippage. Price over $10 keeps the focus on institutional-grade names. 06 · Episodic Pivots

Trading Episodic Pivots (EPs)

When a fundamental catalyst changes a stock's story overnight — and how to capitalize on it with precision.

Episodic Pivots represent some of the most explosive opportunities in the market. They occur when a stock experiences a dramatic shift in its fundamental outlook, creating a powerful gap-up on significant volume — not from a chart pattern alone, but from a genuine change in the company's prospects.

The most powerful EPs share a specific anatomy. The stock has spent weeks or months in sideways drift or gradual decline, allowing weak holders to exit and creating a "coiled spring" effect. When the catalyst arrives — a surprise earnings beat, regulatory approval, strategic investment, or major contract — it triggers a violent gap-up that catches short sellers off guard. Institutional investors, now paying attention for the first time, begin building positions and propel the stock further.

Critical filter: Is the stock gapping up just below major overhead resistance, like a 200-day moving average? If so, it's likely not a quality candidate. The best EPs gap into open air.

Scanning Criteria

Before the market opens, systematic scanning for qualifying EPs requires the following pre-gap criteria:

≥10% Minimum Gap Size
4–12w Base Duration
20M+ Min Share Float
≥3% ADR (14-day)
$8+ Min Share Price
No Bio Avoid Biotech Stocks

Volume Confirmation: Pre-market volume is a crucial filter. EPs that trade 1×, 2×, or 3× their Average Daily Volume within the first 15–30 minutes of the regular session are the strongest signals of genuine institutional buying. Light or declining pre-market volume often signals a false breakout that will quickly fade.

Entry Tactics

Five Ways to Enter an Episodic Pivot

If the stock rallies immediately at open, use Entry #1. If it reverses, look for whichever of #2–#5 develops first.

  • 01
    Immediate Momentum Entry Aggressive · For stocks that gap and immediately continue higher

    Enter long when the stock begins moving up from its opening price — but only if the move doesn't exceed 60% of the stock's daily ATR from the low of day. This filter prevents chasing extended moves that lack follow-through. Calculate ATR using the previous 14 days. On a $2.00 ATR stock, do not enter if the stock has already moved more than $1.20 from its opening price. Stop-loss is placed at the low-of-day.

07 · Risk Management

Position Sizing & Selling Rules

The rules that protect capital and lock in profits — applied consistently regardless of conviction level.

Risk management is not an afterthought — it is the primary mechanism that keeps you in the game long enough for the big winners to compound. No single trade, regardless of how compelling the setup, should have the ability to materially damage the portfolio.

0.5–1% Risk Per Trade (Good Markets)
25% Max Position Size (Portfolio)
2×R First De-Risk Target
1/3 Shares Sold at First Target
10 EMA Structure-Based Exit Signal
2 Days Closes Below 10 EMA to Trim

The Selling Framework

The selling discipline operates in stages, with the first sell being purely mechanical. One-third of shares are sold at 2× the initial risk taken — this de-risks the position early, locking in real profits and shifting the psychological pressure off the remaining shares. This first trim is not optional; it happens at the target regardless of how strong the stock looks.

After that initial de-risk, subsequent trims are structure-based rather than price-target-based. The primary signal is two consecutive daily closes below the 10 EMA. This tells you the stock's momentum has shifted and that the institutional bid that was supporting every dip is weakening. Rather than holding blindly to a fixed percentage target, you're reading the stock's actual behavior and responding to what it's telling you.

The overriding philosophy is that the first trim is mechanical and fast — it removes the risk of giving back a winner. Everything after that is managed with patience, letting the stock's structure dictate when to reduce further. What is non-negotiable is that some selling occurs as price moves up. This guarantees a portion of profits is secured regardless of what happens next.

Stop-Loss Placement by Trade Type

For 8 EMA structure entries, the initial stop is placed at the structure low, then raised naturally as the stock continues higher, with the 10 EMA serving as the longer-term trailing guide. Two consecutive closes below the 10 EMA trigger a partial or full exit.

For Episodic Pivots, the stop-loss is placed at the low-of-day on the day of the EP. This level represents the market's real-time verdict on the catalyst — a violation of that low signals the gap is being rejected and the trade should be exited.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid: Never enter stocks that have already moved more than 60% of their daily ATR from the opening price. Avoid gaps on light volume or without a clear fundamental catalyst. Never ignore the Trend M.A.P. reading — even the most textbook EP setup carries substantially higher failure risk when the broader market is in a RED or ORANGE condition. Avoid biotech stocks entirely due to the binary risk of overnight gap-downs of 80–90%.

How the Three Layers Work Together

The complete decision framework — from market open to position management.

The Daily Decision Flow

🌊
Step 1 — Assess the Trend M.A.P. Before any stock analysis, run the seven-point QQQE health checklist. Determine today's signal color. This tells you whether to be in max-aggression mode, cautious mode, or flat in cash. No exceptions.
📊
Step 2 — Confirm the Stock is in 8 EMA Phase The stock must have already broken out with conviction and be surfing the 8 EMA. Look for shallowing pullbacks, contracting volume on dips, and price staying above the 8 EMA. This is the phase where institutional urgency creates the most reliable follow-through.
⚡
Step 3 — Check for Episodic Pivot Conditions Pre-market, scan for qualifying EPs: 10%+ gap, strong volume, clear fundamental catalyst, proper base structure, no overhead resistance. Rank candidates by quality of catalyst and volume character.
🎯
Step 4 — Select Entry Tactic For EPs: try Entry #1 (Immediate Momentum) if the stock rallies at open; otherwise watch for #2–#5. For 8 EMA structures: wait for contracting volume on the pullback, then buy the expansion back above the average on increasing volume.
🛡️
Step 5 — Size the Position and Place the Stop Use the Risk Manager tool to calculate shares based on distance from entry to stop. For EPs: risk 0.25–0.5% of account. For swing trades: up to 1%. Never exceed 25% of portfolio in any single position.
📈
Step 6 — Manage the Position First sell: 1/3 of shares at 2× initial risk — mechanical, no exceptions. Subsequent trims are structure-based: two consecutive daily closes below the 10 EMA signal the momentum has shifted and it's time to reduce. Let the stock's behavior, not a fixed price target, dictate when to trim further.
🔁
Step 7 — Use Personal Results as Feedback Are trades gaining traction? If yes — press the best setups. If no — reduce size, become more selective, or pause entirely. Your P&L is itself a real-time market health signal.

The Unifying Principle: The 8 EMA entry gives you a ticket onto a train that has already proven its destination — after the breakout, when the stock's institutional backing is no longer in question. Episodic Pivots catch the moments when the journey begins in a single, explosive day. The Trend M.A.P. tells you whether the winds are right to sail at all. Together, they form a complete, repeatable system — one that is aggressive when conditions are right and disciplined when they are not.

Step-by-Step Walkthrough

How to Add a Custom Script in TradingView

This only needs to be done once per script. After that, the indicator lives in your TradingView account and can be added to any chart in a single click.

1
Download the script file Click one of the download buttons above. A .txt file will save to your Downloads folder. Open it with any text editor (Notepad, TextEdit, VS Code) and select all the text, then copy it.
2
Open a chart on TradingView Navigate to any chart. It doesn't matter which ticker — you'll be adding the indicator to your account, not just this one chart.
3
Open the Pine Script Editor At the bottom of the chart, click the Pine Editor tab. A code panel will open. If you don't see it, look for the </> icon in the bottom toolbar.
4
Clear the editor and paste the code The editor will have some placeholder code already in it. Select all of it (Ctrl+A on Windows, Cmd+A on Mac) and delete it. Then paste the copied script code.
5
Click "Add to chart" In the top-right of the Pine Editor panel, click the Add to chart button. The indicator will immediately appear on your chart. If you see a compile error instead, make sure no extra characters were added when pasting.
6
Save it to your Indicators library Click the Save button (floppy disk icon) and give it a name. Once saved, you can find it anytime under Indicators → My Scripts on any chart — no need to paste the code again.
7
Configure the settings Double-click the indicator name on the chart (or click the ⚙ gear icon) to open its Settings panel. For the ATR Line script, set the ATR Percentage to 60 to match the system's entry rule. For the MA Structure script, set your preferred MA length and type under the Moving Average Settings group.
